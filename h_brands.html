Handicraft industry

Local handicraft brands

D&E Mats and Rattan Crafts

Mrs. Elvira Padoc established D&E Mats and Rattan Crafts in 1983. Making handmade embroidered bags and mats was a passion that evolved into a company.

They have access to neighborhood pasalubong shops, other MSMEs, and institutional customers in terms of market reach. They have marketed their goods in regional, national, and local trade shows. They already know the fundamentals of exporting and already have an established organizational structure and constant access to raw materials.

Eva Marie Arts and Crafts

Eva Marie Arts and Crafts is owned and operated by Ms. Eva Marie Adona-Yu of Basey, Samar. She started the business in 2011.

The Philippines and other countries are established markets for their company. They took the trip to Canada in 2022 to introduce their handcrafted bags to the North American market. They market their goods locally, provincially, regionally, and nationally in addition to online

Basey Association of Native Industry Growth (BANIG)

A year after Typhoon Haiyan, the Basey Association for Native Industry Growth (BANIG) was established. With the aid of DTI Samar, the local weavers' associations came together to form BANIG.

They have accessibility to regional, national, and local markets. Local boutiques, as well as department and retail stores, are all included. Additionally, they collaborate with other MSMEs, the provincial government, and a platform for internet marketing.

Capangihan Weavers Association

The Barangay Capangihan, Tolosa, is the home of the Capangihan Weavers Association. The association has employed mother weavers to produce indigenous handicrafts like as mats, purses, wallets, home decor, slippers, bayong, and other materials made of tikog grass for the past seven years.

Local farmers provide them with anahaw, tikog, and buri leaves. Their goods have been sold all around the nation. Most participate in live trade fairs and bazaars in addition to selling at pasalubong locations.

Clarita Villamor, also as Clair by many, was born and raised in Tacloban. She started "Clair's Enterpises" in the 1990s because she is passionate about weaving. She comes from a weaving family. Both of her parents and her grandfather were weavers. They make mats, bags, slippers, handbags, and other home design items out of tikog, which they use as a raw material.

They get their basic materials in Leyte's Tanauan and San Miguel. They participate in trade shows and utilize pasalubong centers to sell locally, regionally, and nationally.
