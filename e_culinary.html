Background

So, you're probably wondering what's the point of that mini-survey. Well, culinary arts is not just about cooking; it's also about creativity, innovation, and discovering new flavors. Through our mini-survey, we aim to showcase the diverse and exciting flavors of Filipino cuisine.

Culinary arts, also known as cuisine or cooking, is the practice of preparing and presenting food in the most creative way. The Escoffier School of Culinary Arts believes that cooking is taken to a higher level of sophistication, creativity, and expression in the world of culinary arts. It involves the use of skills, imagination, and intuition to create something unique and meaningful, as well as the sensory experience of taste, smell, and sight. They can evoke powerful emotions and memories, and can create feelings of nostalgia, joy, or comfort.

Culinary Arts and the Economy

The Philippine culinary arts sector contributes to job expansion and cultural tourism. The country's culinary heritage draws influences from Spanish, Chinese, Malay, and indigenous cultures. Binondo, Manila's Chinatown, is a popular destination for culinary tourism, known for its vibrant Chinese-Filipino cuisine. Wong et al. (2021) found that foreign tourists consistently rated their experience in Binondo positively each year. The Department of Tourism (DOT) is responsible for promoting the country's tourism industry, including its cultural heritage sites and culinary tourism, which generates tourism activity and economic growth, as mandated by the Tourism Act of 2009.

As tourists (both domestic and international) visit a place to explore its food culture and cuisine, it creates a demand for local food products and services. In return, this generates job opportunities for people living there, including farmers, servers, and chefs. Local farmers and producers profit from increasing local food demand, which boosts the local economy by bringing more money in. The rise in demand for local food products and services can have a tremendous impact on the culinary industry's output. With the increasing demand for culinary tourism, businesses tend to allocate more investments toward the expansion of their services, leading to a rise in employment and production. By means of this, it has the potential to significantly sway our GDP by boosting economic expansion through a surge in purchasing authority. This shows the potential economic growth-stimulating abilities of culinary arts, not just for the food business but also for other sectors.

The contribution of the culinary arts industry to the economy, especially through tourism and job opportunities, is unprecedented. A growing number of tourists worldwide visit the Philippines with the desire to experience our exquisite yet affordable cuisine, in line with an escalating trend in culinary tourism. Ignoring and falling short in endorsing our culinary arts can lead to lost opportunities that could have stimulated economic growth by way of tourism and its associated industries.

Promoting Culinary Arts

Social media platforms like TikTok, Instagram, Youtube, and Facebook have become popular in the Culinary Arts industry. Food vloggers and enthusiasts dominate these platforms, showcasing local dishes in visually appealing videos. This promotes diverse culinary offerings to a wider audience, both local and international.

Food vloggers often feature affordable yet delicious food options, which can impact consumer behavior. They help promote local businesses with quality meals at reasonable prices, stimulating economic growth. Without them, small food businesses may struggle to gain visibility and attract customers. In addition, without their creative and engaging content, many people may not be aware of the diverse and delicious culinary offerings available in the Philippines.
