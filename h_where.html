Handicraft industry

Where to go?

Filipinos are known for their creativity and resourcefulness since, Philippines has a lot natural resources and we can come up with a lot of things in all of this; Handicrafts are one of the craft-making filipinos are known for it also preserves the culture of the provinces in the Philippines, to get to know more here are the lists of known handicrafts in the Philippines.

ILOCOS, SUR

Burnay

Burnay is a pottery-work in the shape of pots and jars, It is an earthenware jar crafted by potters that used Grade A clay. This material is available in Western Area of Vigan. Before, Burnay are only used to store foods such as Kaong (sugar palm tree), salt, fermented fish, etc. but now, it is also seen as a design for households.

As of 2013, there are only 3 Burnay Jars factories remaining: RG Jar, Ruby Pottery, and NP Jar factory all located in Vigan, Ilocos Sur

ILOCOS REGION

Inabel Fabric

“Abel” is an Ilocano word for “weave” and Inabel can also stand for “woven fabric”. This handicraft is a pride of the Ilocos Region that is made of cotton known for being soft, strength, and beautiful design.

Inabel has different kinds of Design Technique: Binakul Pattern, Pinilian Weave, and Ikat Tie Dye

IFUGAO PROVINCE, CORDILLERA

Bahag

Bahag is a traditional loincloth of Indigenous people commonly worn by Men from Cordillera Region or known as “Igorot”. It is made from hand-woven textiles that come with a variety of rich colors.

“Isneg” an ethnic group native from Apayao wears Blue bahag, “Ibaloi” an indigenous group from Benguet province wears sport white and, “Kankanaey” an indigenous group also from Benguet provinces wears blue and red designs.

BASEY, SAMAR

Banig

Banig is a handwoven mat that is known in East Asia and also in the Philippines, it is used for sitting and sleeping. Basey, Samar is considered as the “Banig Capital of the Philippines” as it continues to flourish in that place they called the women weavers “paraglara”

Banig is usually made from “Tikog” (special reed grass”) it usually measures 2x3 meters thin as clipboard and the mat has colorful patterns.
